Background Information

According to recent data, youths aged 18-29 are the largest population subgroup reporting poor mental health. Goh (2023) highlighted that this group makes up 25.3% of mental health cases, with high informal help-seeking behavior. The National Population Health Survey 2024 revealed that 55% of youths lack sufficient muscle-strengthening activity, while obesity rates in the 18-28 bracket have nearly doubled from 6.6% to 11.6% (Qiang, 2025).

With most medical health institutions in singapore being more focused on the elderly due to the ageing society, less attention and resources have been placed on our youths. More and more Active Ageing Centers are being built and funded while youth initiatives have been pushed towards teenagers and early childhood. This leaves little community resources for young adult aged 18 to 28 to tap into when they require help.

The need for physical and mental wellness advocation programs like the NHG Youth Lab is more crucial than ever to ensure a sustainable, healthy future.

Our Mission

As Singapore moves towards Healthier SG, the focus on preventive care has never been more vital. NHG Youth Lab is a national-level hackathon designed to bridge the gap between student innovation and professional healthcare delivery. We challenge participants to look beyond the screen and build tools that foster long-term physical vitality.

NHG Youth Lab

In partnership with the National Healthcare Group (NHG), this hackathon provides the tools, mentorship, and training needed to build the next generation of solutions. This hackathon involves deep youth interaction with their local communities to identify pressing needs and engineer meaningful solutions.

Held around the month of June, the three day event provides crash courses on the current state of youths in Singapore, the current supports provided, and some tools participants can leverage on for more information. With a direct line of contact to NHG representatives and mentors, participants can help clarify requirements and doubts about the programme.

Judges from both NYC and NHG will come in on the last day to conduct the judging rounds. The judges would judge based on 3 criterias: problem identification, solution feasibility, and creativity of the solution.
